# NXY Initial Market Structure and Pricing Simulation

Version: 1.0  
Status: Informational  
Applies to: Token Launch and Exchange Listing  

------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. Overview

This section provides a structured simulation of the initial market pricing framework for the Naira Index (NXY) token.

The purpose of this section is to define a rational and institutionally grounded approach to initial token pricing, circulating supply, and market capitalization at the point of centralized exchange (CEX) listing.

The NXY token represents a tokenized benchmark layer and its market price is determined by supply and demand dynamics in the digital asset market.

The token price is not derived directly from the benchmark index value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Pricing Model

The initial token price is determined using the standard market relationship:

Price = Market Capitalization / Circulating Supply

Where:

Price = Initial listing price per NXY token  
Market Capitalization = Total value of circulating tokens at listing  
Circulating Supply = Number of tokens available in the market at launch  

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Total Supply

The NXY protocol defines a fixed maximum supply:

Total Supply = 100,000,000,000 NXY

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Circulating Supply at Launch

A controlled circulating supply is required to support price stability and orderly market formation.

Recommended initial circulating supply:

5% of total supply

Circulating Supply = 5,000,000,000 NXY

This level of circulating supply reduces initial sell pressure and supports efficient price discovery.

300,000 USD to 1,000,000 USD

1,000,000 USD or higher

## 9. Market Positioning

The NXY token represents a tokenized benchmark layer rather than a direct representation of the index value.

The benchmark value (NXY index level) and the token market price are independent variables.

The index provides a macroeconomic reference, while the token enables market participation, liquidity formation, and distribution of the benchmark layer.

## 11. Circulating Supply Composition

The initial circulating supply is derived from specific allocation categories within the token distribution framework.

The 5% circulating supply at launch is expected to originate from:

Liquidity allocation  
Exchange distribution allocation  
Market-making reserves  

Founding, strategic, and treasury allocations are not included in the initial circulating supply and are subject to vesting and lock-up conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12. Vesting and Lock-Up Policy

To support market stability and prevent excessive early supply pressure, certain token allocations are subject to vesting and lock-up restrictions.

These include:

Founding management and staff allocation  
Strategic investment allocation  
Treasury allocation  

Vesting schedules may include:

Cliff periods  
Linear unlock mechanisms  
Time-based release schedules  

These measures ensure gradual supply introduction and alignment of long-term incentives.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13. Token Release Schedule

Token supply expansion beyond the initial circulating supply follows a controlled release schedule.

The release of tokens into circulation is governed by:

Vesting schedules  
Governance-approved allocations  
Liquidity provisioning requirements  

The objective is to ensure orderly market development and minimize supply shocks.
